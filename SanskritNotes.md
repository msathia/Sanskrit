## Grammar Notes

### अच्सन्धिः / अच्-सन्धिः

- `अच्` means vowels: अ, आ, इ, ई, उ, ऊ, ऋ, ॠ, ऌ, ए, ऐ, ओ, औ.
- अच्सन्धिः is vowel sandhi: changes that happen when one vowel comes next to another vowel, usually at a word boundary or morpheme boundary.
- Basic idea: final vowel + initial vowel may combine into a long vowel, गुण, वृद्धि, or glide form depending on the pair.
- Common first pattern: सवर्णदीर्घसन्धिः, where same-class vowels combine into the corresponding long vowel.
  - अ + अ/आ -> आ: राम + अवतारः -> रामावतारः
  - इ/ई + इ/ई -> ई: गिरि + ईशः -> गिरीशः
  - उ/ऊ + उ/ऊ -> ऊ: गुरु + उपदेशः -> गुरूपदेशः
- When reading, split a sandhi form by asking: which two vowels could have met here?
