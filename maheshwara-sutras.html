महेश्वरसूत्राणि

A compact sound-map used to form प्रत्याहाराः such as अच्, हल्, इक्, and यण्. This page keeps sound groups and marker letters separate from sandhi rule applications.

Core Idea

प्रत्याहारः

start letter + marker letter
= compact name for a group of sounds

The final letter of each sutra is an इत् marker. It helps form the group name but is not counted as a normal sound inside that group.

हलन्तशब्दः

हल् + अन्तः
= ending in a consonant

A हलन्तशब्दः is a consonant-ending word or stem, such as जगत्, भवत्, or राजन्.

Sound Classification

This is the study grouping. It is different from the Maheshwara sutra order, whose main job is to form compact प्रत्याहार names.

Top Level Class Sounds Sutra Memory
Vowels Simple vowels अ आ, इ ई, उ ऊ, ऋ ॠ, ऌ The sutras show the short bases: अ इ उ ऋ ऌ.
Vowels Diphthongs / compound vowels ए ओ ऐ औ ए ओ are गुण vowels; ऐ औ are वृद्धि vowels.
Consonants ह + semivowels ह्, य् र् ल् व् हयवरट् and लण्. Only य् र् ल् व् are semivowels.
Consonants Nasals, 5th of each varga ङ् ञ् ण् न् म् Sutra order: ञ म ङ ण न.
Consonants Soft aspirates, 4th of each varga घ् झ् ढ् ध् भ् Sutra order: झ भ, then घ ढ ध.
Consonants Soft unaspirates, 3rd of each varga ग् ज् ड् द् ब् Sutra order: ज ब ग ड द.
Consonants Hard aspirates, 2nd of each varga ख् छ् ठ् थ् फ् Sutra order: ख फ छ ठ थ.
Consonants Hard unaspirates, 1st of each varga क् च् ट् त् प् Sutra order: च ट त, then क प.
Consonants Sibilants and ह् श् ष् स् ह् श ष स र् plus ह्.

Varga Grid

Within each consonant class, columns 1 and 2 are hard; columns 3 and 4 are soft; the 5th column is nasal. Here hard means voiceless, soft means voiced, and “+ breath” means aspirated or महाप्राण. Traditional labels: hard = अघोष / कठोर; soft = सघोष / मृदु.

Hard / Voiceless

अघोष literally means “without resonance.” The vocal cords do not vibrate; the sound is made by a crisp release of breath.

क् ख् च् छ् ट् ठ् त् थ् प् फ्

These feel sharper, so they are remembered as कठोर, hard.

Soft / Voiced

सघोष means “with resonance.” The vocal cords vibrate, creating a deeper and more resonant sound.

ग् घ् ज् झ् ड् ढ् द् ध् ब् भ्

These feel gentler in the throat, so they are remembered as मृदु, soft.

Throat Check

Place your fingers flat against the throat or larynx. Say क: you feel the breath release, but no throat vibration. Say ग: you should feel a buzzing vibration under the fingers.

हल्

हल् means all consonants. Start at ह in the fifth sutra and go up to the final ल् marker in the fourteenth sutra.

The final ल् in हल् is the marker. It is not counted there. The consonant ल् is still included because it appears earlier in लण्. The sound ह् appears at both ends of the traversal, but for the normal consonant list it is counted once.

Useful Pratyaharas

अच्

अ ... च्
= अ इ उ ऋ ऌ ए ओ ऐ औ

All vowels. This is why vowel sandhi is called अच्सन्धिः.

इक्

इ ... क्
= इ उ ऋ ऌ

This group is useful when remembering यण्सन्धिः.

यण्

य ... ण्
= य व र ल

The semivowels used in यण्सन्धिः: इ -> य्, उ -> व्, ऋ -> र्, ऌ -> ल्.

हल्

ह ... ल्
= all consonants

This is the source behind हलन्तशब्दः.
